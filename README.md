### About the Experiment 

This experiment focuses on the fundamental transport mechanisms of osmosis and diffusion, which are critical for understanding how substances move across biological membranes to maintain cellular homeostasis. Through the simulation, users can manipulate variables such as solute concentration, temperature, and membrane permeability to observe their effects on the rate of molecular movement.  By providing a visual and quantitative representation of concentration gradients and osmotic pressure, the lab allows students to explore how water moves from areas of low solute concentration to high solute concentration and how molecules spread until equilibrium is reached. This virtual environment is essential for biomedical students to grasp the physiological principles underlying processes like kidney dialysis, nutrient absorption, and cellular hydration without the need for traditional wet-lab setups.
